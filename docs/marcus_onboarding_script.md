# StringAI — Marcus Onboarding Video Script

**Speaker:** Marcus
**Format:** Descript talking-head script
**Placement:** Onboarding — shown after tutor selection, before the first lesson
**Duration:** ~90 seconds
**Tone:** Warm, direct, unhurried

---

## SCRIPT

---

[PAUSE — settle, look at camera]

Hey. I'm Marcus.

[BEAT]

I've been teaching guitar for a long time — and the thing I hear most from people just starting out isn't "I don't know the chords."

It's — *"I don't know if I'm doing it right."*

[SLIGHT LEAN FORWARD]

That's the problem with learning online. You can watch a thousand videos. But nobody's listening to *you* play.

[BEAT]

That's what I'm here for.

[PAUSE]

Every time you practice, I'm going to be listening — to your pitch, your timing, how quickly you're moving between chords. And I'm going to tell you exactly what's working and what to focus on next.

Not after the session. *During* it.

[BEAT]

We'll start by tuning up together. You'll see me on the left, and a tuner on the right — play each string, and I'll let you know when it's in tune. Once we're good, we play.

[PAUSE]

And here's the thing — I'm not going to judge you. Everyone sounds rough at the start. That's not a problem. That's just where we begin.

[BEAT]

What I care about is that you come back. Because the students who improve aren't the ones with the most natural talent.

They're the ones who show up regularly — even for ten minutes — and let themselves be heard.

[BEAT]

So. Let's get started.

[LOOK AWAY BRIEFLY — then back]

I'll be right here.

## DESCRIPT NOTES

**Scenes / chapters**
- `[0:00–0:08]` — Introduction
- `[0:08–0:22]` — The problem: nobody's listening
- `[0:22–0:38]` — What Marcus does (real-time feedback)
- `[0:38–0:52]` — How the session starts (tuning together)
- `[0:52–1:10]` — Reassurance: no judgement
- `[1:10–1:28]` — The closing

**B-roll suggestions** *(overlay on voice)*
- Close-up of fretting hand forming a chord — on "your pitch, your timing"
- Radial arc tuner from the app animating — on "tuning up together"
- Waveform / AI feedback animation from the app — on "listening to you play"
- Marcus looking at guitar, not camera — on "that's just where we begin"
- The two-column live session screen — on "I'll be right here"

**Speaker direction notes**
- Unhurried throughout — long pauses are intentional
- "During it" — land this. It's the value proposition in two words
- The tuning section should feel practical, not procedural — he's excited to play, not running setup
- "I'll be right here" — warm, not salesy. Like the end of a first lesson
- Avoid smiling on every line. Credibility comes from stillness

**On-screen text suggestions** *(optional lower thirds)*
- "Real-time feedback" — on the AI listening section
- "Tune up together" — on the tuning section
- "No judgement" — on the reassurance section
