What we're building
A fully AI-powered guitar tutoring platform that hears you play.
Browser-based. No download, no human instructors. The AI listens via the browser microphone and gives real-time feedback on pitch, timing, and chord changes — as you play, not after. Every aspect of the experience adapts continuously across three independent axes.

Research
Competitive analysis
5 platforms reviewed: Yousician, Fender Play, JustinGuitar, TrueFire, ArtistWorks. Assessed across 6 dimensions. No platform combines real-time feedback with multilingual instruction. Emotional adaptation is absent across all five.
Survey · n=10
12 questions, 4 sections. Bias review conducted — 5 questions corrected. Key finding: skill-match scores consistently low (2–3/5) across all personas. AI openness highest among beginners, lowest among advanced learners.
Interviews · n=10
Semi-structured, 20–30 min each, across all 4 personas. Features never suggested — needs surfaced unprompted. Diego: "Online doesn't have ears." Became the core design principle for Practice Mode.
Feasibility assessment
Real-time pitch and timing detection via Web Audio API validated as feasible. Browser-first approach chosen: no app install, no permissions barrier, accessible from any device with a microphone.
No one can hear them play. Every platform is one-directional. The most universal and urgent unmet need across all 4 personas.
Reassurance before evaluation. Post-session summary must function as guidance first, scores second. Applied across the entire product.
Tone adaptation works in both directions. Over-encouragement of advanced players destroys credibility instantly. The system must strip scaffolding as aggressively as it adds warmth.
Classical guitar and non-European markets are underserved. Flagged as strategic expansion directions post-MVP.
Who we're designing for
The Absolute Beginner
"I feel like I'm learning two things at once."
Never played. Needs structure, zero jargon, and frequent reassurance. Scared of forming bad habits with no one to correct them.
The Lapsed Player
"Tell me where I actually am — not where I say I am."
Played years ago, stopped. Doesn't want to start from scratch. The diagnostic is their entire value proposition.
The Intermediate Grinder
"Online doesn't have ears."
Can play songs but has plateaued. Every session without feedback may be reinforcing bad technique.
The Advanced Learner
"It would have to surprise me."
Near-professional. Low tolerance for generic content. Must be proven before trusted. Needs depth, precision, niche expertise.
Core features
Live Practice AI-native · Main feature
The only surface that could not exist without AI as its foundation. The browser microphone captures your playing in real time. Marcus listens, analyses, and responds — not after you finish, but as each note lands.
Real-time pitch, timing, and chord change tracking via browser microphone
Two-column live session: Marcus hand video + chord diagrams left, your camera + mini radial arc metrics right
Marcus adapts: hand closeup when you need to see finger position, portrait when things are going well
Session closure: modal with scores + "Continue practicing" (resumes) or "Next lesson" (advances)
AI Tutor
On-demand conversational tutor, available 24/7. Adapts tone, vocabulary, and complexity to your level and emotional state. Aware of session history and inferred state.
Lesson Player
Structured video curriculum by level and guitar type. Loop sections, slow down to 50/75%, toggle chord diagram overlays. Adapts density and annotation depth by skill level.
Progress — Status: Open Mic
Level names come from the learner's genre — not numbers. Rock: Garage Band → Open Mic → Bar Band → Studio Ready → Rock Star → Legend. Motivational for beginners, analytical for experts.
